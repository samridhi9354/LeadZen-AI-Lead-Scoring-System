✦ Technical Deep Dive

How LeadZen Works Internally

01
Persistence Layer Load
Before inference, the engine checks for lead_classifier.json and pipeline_encoders.pkl. If found, it loads in Zero-Train Mode — guaranteeing the exact 91.5% state.
mode = "PRE-TRAINED LOAD" if models exist
02
Drop-and-Map Preprocessing
Identifiers (Company Name) and labels (Actual Cohort) are removed. Saved LabelEncoders apply .transform() — never .fit_transform() — preventing data leakage.
encoder.transform(col) ← not fit_transform
03
XGBoost Prediction Pass
The model runs predict() and predict_proba() across all transformed leads, returning both hard class labels and probability vectors.
proba[i] = [P(Cold), P(Warm), P(Hot)]
04
Label Mapping & Output
Numeric outputs (0, 1, 2) are mapped to human-readable labels via a label map. Results are appended as a Predicted Cohort column and exported.
{0:"Cold", 1:"Warm", 2:"Hot"}

✦ Cohort Guide

Understanding Your Lead Labels

🔥 Hot Leads — Act Now

Score > 70. Strong ROI signal: high wasted hours, immediate timeline, confirmed budget, and decision-maker contact. These leads convert. Contact within 24 hours.

🌡️ Warm Leads — Nurture

Score 35–70. Interest is present but urgency or budget is unclear. Enter into a 2–3 week automated nurture sequence. Re-evaluate after engagement.

❄️ Cold Leads — Automate

Score < 35. Poor ROI fit or low engagement signals. Don't waste rep time. Drop into a low-touch drip campaign and revisit in 60+ days.

Scoring Formula
raw_score =
  (hours_wasted / 3)
  + (budget / 1000)
  + demo_bonus
  + authority_bonus
  + timeline_bonus

Hot → score > 70
Warm → 35 < score ≤ 70
Cold → score ≤ 35
* Simplified. Actual model uses 22 features with XGBoost non-linear relationships.
